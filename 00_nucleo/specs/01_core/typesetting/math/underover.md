## Prescriptive Axioms

### Axiom I: Manifold Excitation Levels

$$
\text{Energy} \in \{\text{Display (high)}, \text{Text (low)}\}
$$

**Manifold Excitation Levels**: The mathematical manifold exists in different energy states:
- **High energy (Display)**: Expanded, polar configuration
- **Low energy (Text)**: Compact, satellite configuration

---

### Axiom II: Polar Attraction Law

$$
E = \text{high} \Rightarrow \text{limits} \to \text{poles}
$$

**Polar Attraction Law**: In manifolds of high energy (display mode), limits are **attracted to the magnetic poles** of the nucleus — placing them directly above and below.

$$
\text{over} \xrightarrow{\text{pole}_N} \text{above nucleus}
$$
$$
\text{under} \xrightarrow{\text{pole}_S} \text{below nucleus}
$$

---

### Axiom III: Satellite Decay

$$
E = \text{low} \Rightarrow \text{limits} \to \text{satellites}
$$

**Satellite Decay**: In manifolds of low energy (text mode), limits **decay into satellite orbitals** — becoming superscripts and subscripts.

---

### Axiom IV: Centering Invariant

$$
\text{center}(\text{over}) = \text{center}(\text{nucleus}) = \text{center}(\text{under})
$$

In polar configuration, all elements share a **common center axis**.
