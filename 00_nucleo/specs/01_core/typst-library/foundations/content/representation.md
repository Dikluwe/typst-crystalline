## 💎 Facet DNA

$$
\text{Representation} : \text{Atom} \to \text{Physical Form}
$$

**packed.rs**, **raw.rs**, and **vtable.rs** collectively define **Atom Representation** — the physical manifestation of semantic atoms.

---

## Prescriptive Axioms

### Axiom I: Identity Persistence Law

$$
\forall r \in \text{Representations}: \text{topology}(r) = \text{invariant}
$$

**Identity Persistence Law**: The physical form of the atom (compact, erased, or dispatched) **does not alter its semantic topology**.

---

### Axiom II: Representation Modes

$$
\text{Representation} \in \{\text{Compact}, \text{Erased}, \text{Dispatched}\}
$$

Atoms may exist in different **physical forms**:
- **Compact**: Optimized storage with reference sharing
- **Erased**: Dynamic, runtime-resolved form
- **Dispatched**: Operation-ready with resolved behaviors

---

### Axiom III: Form Interchangeability

$$
\text{Compact}(a) \equiv \text{Erased}(a) \equiv a
$$

All representation forms are **semantically equivalent**.
